# WORLD BIBLE

## THE CONTINENT

A single landmass of five distinct biomes radiating from a compressed center. The geography is unnatural—scholars debate whether it is a quirk of tectonics or the scar of some ancient magical working. The Order maintains it is natural. The Order is incorrect.

### West — The Sun-Scorched Expanse

Desert. Ancient, not barren. Canyons carved by rivers that died before recorded history. Salt flats that gleam like mirrors under the twin moons. Mesa-cities honeycombed with wind-catching tunnels and subterranean cisterns. Oases are the only true wealth. The largest—an inland sea called the Gilded Eye—is the heart of the Dominion. The deep desert holds ruins of a prior civilization. Scavengers return with pottery shards that hum.

### North — The Crown of Winter

Snowy mountains. A maze of peaks, passes, and high-altitude plateaus where the cold never fully retreats. Glaciers calve into fjords on the northern coast. Hot springs and volcanic vents provide habitable pockets. Cities are tunneled into stone, sealed against blizzards. The highest peak, the Needle, hosts a small Order observatory—officially for meteorological study, unofficially for reasons no one will confirm.

### East — The Green Vein

Plains. Grassland stretching to the horizon, broken by wide slow rivers and old-growth forest stands. The agricultural engine of the continent. Soil is deep and black. The sky is enormous. Cities sprawl outward in low rings rather than rising upward. The eastern border touches a sea no one crosses—not because it is impassable, but because the fishing is too good close to shore to justify the risk.

### South — The Shattered Archipelago

Maritime. Thousands of islands from volcanic spires to coral atolls to jungle-choked highlands. The sea is both highway and barrier. Shipbuilding is the highest art. Navigation is sacred knowledge. Shallow waters hold visible wrecks with accessible cargo. Deep trenches descend beyond measurement—something old stirs there. The archipelago has no single ruler but a league of port-cities that negotiate as a bloc.

### Center — The Crossroads

All biomes in miniature. Rolling hills yielding to sudden canyons, forest pockets, a salt lake, geothermal springs, a small desert, a smaller mountain range. The Order's headquarters, the Cradle, sits at the exact center—a walled citadel-city, extraterritorial, belonging to no realm. The five realms meet here for diplomacy, trade, and the quiet negotiation of mage-placements.

## THE FIVE REALMS

### WEST — THE KEMET-SUN DOMINION

**Governing Structure:** A theocratic empire ruled by the Sun-Pharaoh, a living god-king claiming descent from the sun itself. The state religion venerates the Five Gates as divine aspects, with Fire (the sun) elevated above all others.

**Public Relationship with the Order:** The Dominion welcomes the Order as a sacred institution that identifies and trains those touched by the divine aspects. The Order is integrated into religious ceremony and state function.

**Quiet Reality:** The Dominion is the Order's oldest and deepest relationship. They receive more placed mages than any other realm—primarily Water and Fire. The Pharaoh's priesthood is quietly dependent on Order-trained Water-mages to maintain the irrigation systems that prevent mass starvation. Donations are exorbitant, paid in gold, gems, and access to desert ruins the Order is extremely interested in. The royal line has attempted three times to breed their own mage bloodline outside Order control. All three attempts produced Sensitives the Order collected before adolescence. A fourth attempt—the Pharaoh's niece, third in line to the throne, Fire-sensitive with an unusual uncatalogued mutation—is currently hidden in a desert monastery staffed by Insensitive monks. The Order suspects but cannot prove she exists.

**Magical Character:**
- Fire-mages: Common (desert heat, sun worship)
- Water-mages: Rare and revered
- Ash mutation: Finest on the continent; the Pharaoh's inquisitors use forensic divination from burned remains
- Affliction: Legal against enemies of the state; heat-theft used for extracting confessions

**Edge Schools Present:**
- Iron Rib: Heavy infantry guarding the oases
- Long Drive: Shock troops for desert raids
- Tortoise: Caravan guards
- Broken Ground: Originated here—desert improvisation, sandstorm tactics, fighting blind

**Key Tension:** If the Order discovers the hidden heir, the Dominion faces exposure and sanctions. The child represents the Pharaoh's last attempt to break free of Order dependency.

### NORTH — THE HOLD-KINGDOMS OF KHAZ-VOR

**Governing Structure:** A confederation of mountain lords who elect a High King during external threats. In peacetime, they govern their own holds independently, bound by ancient oaths of mutual defense. They venerate ancestors, whose bones are interred in the walls of the hold-cities. No gods.

**Public Relationship with the Order:** The Order is tolerated as a necessary institution. Sensitive children are handed over with grim pragmatism, not gratitude.

**Quiet Reality:** The Hold-Kingdoms are in arrears. Three consecutive bad winters have depleted their treasuries. Donations have fallen behind. Mage placements have correspondingly declined—the Order claims coincidence, natural fluctuation in Sensitive births. The mountain lords know better. A faction argues for paying in mineral wealth. Another argues for expelling the Order entirely and training their own Sensitives. A third, growing quietly, argues for building an army of Insensitives. The Order's branch has noticed an unusual number of Insensitive children being withheld from collection. Three noble houses have secretly withheld Sensitive children for two generations, training Bone-mages in the old ways within sealed mountain crypts. These unmarked mages cannot appear in public.

**Magical Character:**
- Earth-mages: Dominant; mountains are their element
- Metal mutation: Common; northern smiths unparalleled
- Ice mutation (Water gate): Second most prevalent; used for construction and defense
- Lightning: Rare but terrifying—a Lightning-mage in a mountain storm is essentially a god
- Bone-mutation: Ancient tradition; ancestors' skeletons animated to defend the holds in times of war. The Order classifies this as "culturally tolerated deviation."

**Edge Schools Present:**
- Tortoise: Mountain defenders holding narrow passes
- White Water: Using vertical terrain for redirection
- Broken Ground: Mountain improvisation
- Last Breath: Originated here—survival techniques when a hold is breached

**Key Tension:** If the Order discovers the hidden Bone-mages, the Hold-Kingdoms must choose between surrendering their ancestral defenders or open rebellion. Neither is survivable.

### EAST — THE GRAIN-LORDS' CONFEDERACY

**Governing Structure:** A decentralized agricultural plutocracy. Land ownership is the only measure of status. Grain-Lords control vast estates worked by tenant farmers and meet in a loose council to manage shared infrastructure and defense. No king. No capital. Power measured in hectares and harvests.

**Public Relationship with the Order:** The Order is treated as a useful service provider—like weather-predictors or irrigation engineers—with transactional courtesy.

**Quiet Reality:** The Confederacy is the Order's most stable, least demanding client. Few mage requests. Payments on time. No questions asked. This makes them the Order's favorite customer, receiving quiet preferential treatment—better-trained mages, faster placement, occasional Savant audits. The Grain-Lords have figured out that being boring is strategic advantage. They harbor the continent's largest population of unregistered Insensitives, not out of ideology but indifference. Some are Savants who chose to spend their lives reading soil debt instead of mage debt—the Order would very much like to know what they are learning about the land. One Grain-Lord, the wealthiest, has never reported his Sensitive grandson. The boy is now fourteen, trained privately by a bribed placed mage and an Unorthodox fighter. A Savant who passed through the estate reported nothing unusual and later accepted a generous land grant. The Order is investigating.

**Magical Character:**
- Wind-mages: Most common; open plains, constant breeze, endless environmental source
- Storm mutation: Dangerously effective; a single Storm-mage can destroy a season's harvest—acts as deterrent against invasion
- Water-mages: Valued for irrigation
- Fire: Rare and distrusted; strict prohibitions against unsanctioned fire-magic during dry seasons
- Breath-mutation (Wind): Primary placed mages; serve as medics clearing lung-diseases

**Edge Schools Present:**
- Grey Veil: Situational awareness across vast flat terrain
- Long Drive: Cavalry charges across open ground
- Grey Herring: Elaborate, ritualized dueling culture among Grain-Lords

**Key Tension:** The soil Savants are discovering something about the land that predates human settlement—something the Order's archives do not explain. And the hidden grandson represents a growing willingness among the elite to circumvent the Order quietly.

### SOUTH — THE MAELSTROM LEAGUE

**Governing Structure:** A coalition of port-cities and island-nations bound by trade. Decisions are made by the Admiralty Council—captains of the largest trading fleets and mayors of the wealthiest ports. The League controls the sea lanes. Their navy is the continent's largest.

**Public Relationship with the Order:** The League maintains Order branches in every major port and treats them with polite suspicion and aggressive negotiation.

**Quiet Reality:** The League is the Order's problem client. They possess wealth to outbid any realm but refuse to play the donation game. Instead, they have invested heavily in a naval academy training maritime-combat Edge specialists specifically to counter mages. They have quietly recruited escaped mages—seven in total, forming a small deniable corps of Order-untrained magical assets. The Order knows about three. One of these escapees, a Water-mage who fled a decade ago, has begun teaching others to guard their reservoirs without Order methods. If this knowledge spreads, the Order's training monopoly collapses. The League's treaty with the Order explicitly limits Savant access to "public facilities"—a clause the admirals wrote themselves.

**Magical Character:**
- Water-mages: Overwhelmingly dominant
- Blood mutation: More common here than anywhere; naval battles are brutal, and the temptation to boil an enemy crew's blood rather than sink the ship (preserving salvage) has produced a generation of technically criminal Blood-mages
- Ice mutation: Reinforcing ship hulls, creating temporary bridges between vessels
- Lightning: Second most prevalent
- Magnet-mutation: Invaluable for navigation and defense against metal weapons
- Pulse-mages: The continent's only; living communication relays between ships at sea

**Edge Schools Present:**
- White Water: Flow and redirection on unstable decks
- Red Coin: Decisive strikes in close-quarters boarding actions
- Broken Ground: Improvisation using ship elements—rigging, booms, cargo
- Naval adaptations of every School; League marines are the finest close-quarters fighters on the continent

**Key Tension:** The hidden mages grow in confidence and number. If the Order proves their existence, the League faces sanctions. If the unmarked Water-mage's training methods spread, the Order's monopoly ends.

### CENTER — THE CRADLE (Order Headquarters)

**Governing Structure:** Extraterritorial enclave governed by the Order under treaty with all five realms. The Order hosts and mediates the Pentagonal Council—the only forum where all five realms meet in peace—but does not vote.

**Structure:**
- Outer Ring: Embassies, trade halls, public temples to the Five Gates
- Middle Ring: Training halls, dormitories, assessment chambers, the Silk Wards where new Sensitives are held
- Inner Ring: The Curators' council chamber, the Savant archives, the vault of debt-records
- Beneath the Vault: Something else. Only senior Curators are cleared to know what.

**Public Face:** The Cradle is a sanctuary where Sensitive children are brought for assessment and training before placement. It is neutral, humanitarian, above politics.

**Quiet Reality:** The Cradle is a fortress disguised as a sanctuary. Every Sensitive brought here is cataloged, assessed, and assigned a debt-rating before adolescence. The Savant council reads every child; their assessments are stored in archives predating all five realms. The Curators know more about the magical demographics of the continent than any realm's government. They use this information to maintain equilibrium—ensuring no realm grows strong enough to challenge the Order, no realm grows desperate enough to attempt alternative training, no realm stops sending donations. Neutrality is genuine. The Order's only loyalty is to the Order.

**Key Tension:** Something beneath the Inner Ring is not a metaphor. It is sleeping. The Order has spent its entire existence ensuring it stays that way. The reason the geography is unnatural. The reason the Cradle was built here.

## MAGIC: THE DEBT FRAMEWORK

*Note: This framework is an Order-authored document. It is the world's understanding of magic as the Order teaches it. It is true as far as the Order knows. It is not complete.*

### First Principles

**What Magic Is:** Magic is borrowing. The universe exists in equilibrium. To work magic is to disturb that equilibrium intentionally—taking something from one place or body and putting it somewhere else. The mage does not create. The mage redirects. Every working leaves a deficit somewhere.

### The Two Reservoirs

Every mage has access to two sources of their element:

**Internal Reservoir:** The element already present in the mage's own body.
- Fire: Body heat, blood-warmth, metabolic fire
- Water: Blood, interstitial fluid, cellular water
- Wind: Breath in lungs, gas exchange, vocal pressure
- Earth: Minerals in bone and blood, bio-potential
- Lightning: Nerve signals, muscle impulses, bioelectricity

The internal reservoir is always available but finite. Deplete it too far, and the body consumes itself. Replenishment is natural but slow.

**Environmental Sources:** The same element present outside the mage's body—ambient heat, standing water, moving air, soil and stone, atmospheric charge. Environmental borrowing is more powerful but not always available. A mage without access to their environmental source is reduced to their internal reservoir alone.

### The Debt Principle

Every working incurs a debt. The debt is the deficit left behind. It must be paid. This is inviolable.

Payment takes three forms:
- **Environmental Debt:** Taken from the environment. Disperses naturally over time. Large debts can leave permanent landscape scars.
- **Self Debt:** Taken from the mage's own body. Paid immediately and personally.
- **Affliction Debt:** Taken from another living creature. The victim manifests the deficit. Affliction is treated as a distinct category of magical assault with harsher penalties.

### The Ledger in Flesh

Debt that is not immediately paid accumulates. The body remembers. Every mage bears physical markers of unpaid debt—the permanent record of every working that took more than was replenished.

- Fire-mages: Frost-scars on fingers, permanent numbness, bone-deep chill
- Water-mages: Cracked lips, dry eyes, unquenchable thirst
- Wind-mages: Reduced lung capacity, perpetual shortness of breath
- Earth-mages: Chronic anemia, brittle joints, grey pallor
- Lightning-mages: Tremors, stutters, phantom numbness, neural fog

These markers are legible. A trained observer can read a mage's history in their flesh. A Savant reads with perfect, instinctive precision. The testimony of a Savant is definitive in magical law.

### The Mark

The Order does not brand or tattoo its mages. The mark is the debt-pattern itself. An Order-trained mage carries a recognizable signature—controlled deepening, disciplined debt-management, orderly progression through the Gates curriculum. The pattern says: *this one is ours.*

When mages encounter each other, they read each other instinctively. Order-trained mages recognize each other. They also recognize when a mage is not Order-trained. The absence of the expected pattern is as visible as its presence. There is no passing. There is only avoidance.

### The Five Gates

A mage is born to one Gate and may deepen within it indefinitely. The Order teaches that no mage crosses to another Gate.

**FIRE — Domain of Transformation**
- Internal: Body heat
- Environmental: Ambient heat, sun, geothermal vents
- Debt signature: Local cold, frost
- Base state: Flame manipulation
- Body sense: Heat-sight (infrared perception)
- Kinetic amplifier: Thermal updraft leaps, muscle superheating
- Affliction: Heat-theft (inducing hypothermia)
- Mutations: Light, Warmth, Ash, Forge

**WATER — Domain of Transition**
- Internal: Blood, interstitial fluid
- Environmental: Humidity, standing water, rivers, sap
- Debt signature: Local aridity, thirst
- Base state: Liquid manipulation
- Body sense: Life-reading through blood flow and hydraulic pressure
- Kinetic amplifier: Hydraulic exoskeleton
- Affliction: Desiccation
- Mutations: Ice, Healing, Blood, Poison

**WIND — Domain of Motion and Vibration**
- Internal: Breath in lungs
- Environmental: Moving air, thermal currents, pressure gradients
- Debt signature: Windless silence, vacuum pockets, pressure drops
- Base state: Air manipulation
- Body sense: Pressure-reading
- Kinetic amplifier: Mass-lightening, compressed-air strikes
- Affliction: Breath-stealing, inner-ear disruption
- Mutations: Sound, Silence, Breath, Storm

**EARTH — Domain of Structure and Persistence**
- Internal: Minerals in bone and blood
- Environmental: Soil, stone, root systems, seismic hum
- Debt signature: Brittle ground, sickening plants, bone-ache
- Base state: Stone/soil manipulation
- Body sense: Tremor-sense
- Kinetic amplifier: Stone-flesh integration
- Affliction: Leaching petrification, calcium theft
- Mutations: Metal, Flesh, Root, Bone

**LIGHTNING — Domain of Signal and Impulse**
- Internal: Bioelectricity
- Environmental: Atmospheric charge, static, storm potential
- Debt signature: Dead zones of charge, neural fog
- Base state: Arc manipulation
- Body sense: Electroreception
- Kinetic amplifier: Neural overclocking
- Affliction: Neural disruption
- Mutations: Nerve, Magnet, Pulse, Static

### Guarding

A mage can actively prevent their internal reservoir from being borrowed. This is a conscious partitioning of attention, not a separate spell. Full guarding prevents all casting. Split attention degrades both guarding and casting. The untrained cannot guard at all—this is why untrained Sensitives are so vulnerable.

### Universal Rules (Per the Order)

1. The debt cannot be cheated.
2. The debt cannot be transferred indefinitely.
3. The mage is a conduit, not a source.
4. The body remembers.
5. The debt always settles.

## THE INSENSITIVE AND THE SENSITIVE

### The Insensitive

Born with a total absence of magical conductivity. They cannot work magic. They cannot be worked upon—Affliction passes through them without effect. Their immunity is permanent and effortless. They have no reservoir to lock, no guard to drop.

**Savants:** A subset of the Insensitive (approximately one in twelve) who can read debt with perfect, instinctive precision. Where a trained physician sees frost-scars and guesses, a Savant sees the full ledger—every deepening, every unpaid balance, every Affliction transferred. Their testimony is definitive in magical law. Mages who wish to hide their history fear Savants above all others.

Savants occupy a strange position—indispensable to the legal system, feared by mages, often isolated from both magical and non-magical communities. They are paid well, housed separately, and rarely trusted.

**Silencers:** Insensitives trained as warriors specifically to counter mages. Immune to magic, deadly at mundane range. They walk through flame bolts and lightning arcs without flinching and respond with steel. The single greatest threat a mage can face.

### The Sensitive

Any untrained magical child who has not yet learned to guard their internal reservoir. All mages are born Sensitive. The distinction is purely developmental.

A Sensitive's unguarded reservoir leaks information. A trained mage can sense another trained mage's gate, depth, and approximate debt only through close contact or active reading. An untrained child broadcasts constantly, visible to anyone who knows how to look.

A Sensitive left alone is safe. A Sensitive left alone in a world with predatory mages is not.

### The Order's Collection

The Order collects Sensitives. This is not a secret. The Order has legal authority to remove untrained magical children from their families in every major jurisdiction. The justification: an untrained Sensitive is a danger to themselves and everyone around them—not because the child will spontaneously work magic, but because someone else will work magic on them.

The Order binds Sensitives in white silk. Silk does not chafe. Silk does not leave marks. A child bound in silk can be transported without visible injury, without evidence of coercion.

Some Sensitives are trained. Some are contained. None are released except by accident or escape.

### The Iron Rule

Every mage must be Order-trained. Every mage must carry the Order's debt-pattern. Any mage operating outside the Order's authority is illegal by definition. Apprehend if possible. Kill if they resist. No exceptions.

The Order presents this as safety—an untrained mage is dangerous, an incorrectly trained mage might have learned heresies. The five realms enforce it as a condition of continued mage-placements. A realm that harbors unmarked mages loses access to marked ones.

### Sensitivity and Social Status

Sensitivity does not respect bloodlines. The child of a queen is exactly as likely to be Sensitive as the child of a dockworker. This is the Order's quietest power. When a royal heir, a noble scion, or the child of a powerful merchant lord is identified as Sensitive, the Order collects them. These children are not mistreated. They are educated, trained, marked. They are also *held*. Their families know that defiance of the Order will affect their child's future. The Order never makes explicit threats. The situation is the threat.

A highborn Sensitive serves the Order twice: as leverage ensuring their family's compliance, and as talent—educated, socially skilled, politically connected, useful for placement in realms where family connections matter.

## MARTIAL POWER: THE EDGE FRAMEWORK

*Note: Like the Debt Framework, this is an Order-informed document. It is the world's understanding of martial craft as systematically taught. It is true as far as it goes.*

### The Martial Principle

Magic is borrowing. Martial power is application. The warrior takes what already exists—mass, momentum, leverage, timing, the mechanical limits of the human body—and applies it with precision. Nothing is borrowed. Nothing is owed.

### The Three Foundations

- **Condition:** Physical capacity—strength, speed, endurance, flexibility, recovery. Built through effort. Lost through neglect.
- **Technique:** Specific patterns of movement and decision that convert condition into effective action. Learned, drilled, internalized.
- **Judgement:** Capacity to apply the right technique at the right moment. Cultivated through experience, reflection, and surviving mistakes.

### The Edge

The trained warrior's advantage over the untrained. Not supernatural. The cumulative difference between trained application and untrained flailing. Manifested in faster strikes (no wasted motion), reading attacks (catching tells unconsciously), steady breath (economy of motion), calm under pressure (familiarity).

### The Limit Principle

The Edge has limits. A warrior cannot cut through plate steel with a longsword. Cannot fight indefinitely. Cannot ignore a mortal wound. When the body fails, it fails. There is no reserve of borrowed power to tap. This is the crucial asymmetry with mages—a mage can overdraw past safety. A warrior cannot.

### Warriors Against Mages

A warrior's Edge does not negate magic. But a warrior who understands the debt system fights the mage's stamina, not their power. Evasion, cover, and pressure force the mage to spend. Every flame bolt dodged is debt the mage must pay. Every moment the warrior stays alive depletes the mage's internal reservoir.

### The Collective Edge

Warriors reinforce each other. A single warrior against a mage is weighted toward the mage. Ten warriors is a problem. A hundred is a conclusion. This is why mages do not rule alone. This is why kingdoms still field armies. Magic is scarce. The Edge is common. In the long run, the common wins.

## THE SCHOOLS OF EDGE

The Schools are pedagogical lineages—specific approaches to training condition, technique, and judgement passed down through generations. No initiation. No oath. Only training.

### The Steel Schools (Condition-Focused)

**Iron Rib School**
- Core: Strength and endurance through graduated resistance
- Signature: The Rooted Guard—absorbing and redirecting force through skeletal alignment
- Weakness: Limited mobility in full guard
- Common among: Heavy infantry, siege defenders, bodyguards

**Long Drive School**
- Core: Explosive power and continuous forward pressure
- Signature: The Cascade—committed strikes feeding momentum into each other
- Weakness: High energy consumption
- Common among: Shock troops, breach-assault specialists, first-blood duelists

**Tortoise School**
- Core: Defensive economy and attrition
- Signature: The Shell Sequence—minimal-motion parry pattern covering all primary attack lines
- Weakness: Limited offensive capacity
- Common among: Caravan guards, rearguard specialists, older fighters

### The Wind Schools (Technique-Focused)

**Grey Herring School**
- Core: Deception and misdirection
- Signature: The False Window—presenting an apparent opening that is a prepared trap
- Weakness: Relies on opponent predictability
- Common among: Duelists, fencing masters, judicial champions

**Red Coin School**
- Core: Speed and precision through economy of motion
- Signature: The Single Exchange—any duel decided in one action, from neutral position, with no telegraph
- Weakness: If the single strike fails, limited follow-up
- Common among: Professional duelists, assassins, honour-guard champions

**White Water School**
- Core: Flow and redirection
- Signature: The Borrowed Force—receiving and redirecting attack energy rather than blocking
- Weakness: Requires opponent commitment; feints provide no purchase
- Common among: Unarmed combat specialists, confined-space bodyguards

### The Shadow Schools (Judgement-Focused)

**Grey Veil School**
- Core: Situational awareness and threat prioritisation
- Signature: The Battlefield Eye—tracking multiple threats, allies, and environmental factors without fixation
- Weakness: Information overload can paralyze
- Common among: Field commanders, banner-guards, skirmish leaders

**Broken Ground School**
- Core: Environmental exploitation and improvisation
- Signature: The Unfair Advantage—systematic use of terrain, objects, and situation
- Weakness: Environment dependency; featureless spaces remove most tools
- Common among: Irregular fighters, scouts, street-brawlers turned professional

**Last Breath School**
- Core: Survival and recovery from imminent defeat
- Signature: The Turn—a trained response to being beaten, creating opportunity from the opponent's overconfidence
- Weakness: Inherently desperation measures
- Common among: Bodyguards, survivors of lost battles, the previously defeated

### The Unorthodox Path

Not a School. The absence of School. Accumulated experience from brawls, mercenary work, battlefield survival, theft of techniques. Characteristics: unpredictable, adaptable, gaps in conditioning, techniques no School would sanction. High variance—some are incompetent, some are as dangerous as any master.

## THE ORDER

### Public Face

The Order saves children from self-destruction. The Order trains them to control their dangerous abilities. The Order returns them to serve their homelands as placed mages. The Order is neutral, humanitarian, above politics. Any realm that denies the Order access is endangering its own children.

### Quiet Reality

The Order curates mages for placement. Donations correlate with quality and quantity. Realms that fall behind find placement numbers dropping. The Order maintains meticulous private records of every mage's capabilities—a catalog never shared. Savants read every mage before placement, giving the Order complete intelligence on every realm's magical assets. The Order does not sell mages. They *place* them as an act of charity that cannot be refused.

### Structure

- **Curators:** Senior leadership. Exact number unknown outside the Inner Ring.
- **Savant Council:** Debt-reading archivists who assess every Sensitive and maintain the archives.
- **Mage-Detectors:** Trained mages who locate unguarded Sensitives.
- **Cradle-Hounds:** Insensitive dogs bred to seek magical signatures. An Order monopoly.
- **Placed Mages:** The finished product—trained, marked, assigned to realms.

### The Cradle's Secret

Something beneath the Inner Ring. Not a metaphor. The senior Curators have known for centuries. It is the reason the Cradle was built here. It is sleeping. The Order ensures it stays that way.

## MAGICAL ARTIFACTS

### Category One: Reservoir Stones

Objects holding elemental charge, pre-loaded for later use. Legal so long as the debt was environmental. Reduce dependency on Order training.

- **Sunstones:** Absorb ambient heat; release on command. Common in Dominion priesthood.
- **Frost-marrow:** Retains cold. Mined in Hold-Kingdoms; smuggled south.
- **Breath-glass:** Traps compressed air. Manufactured in Confederacy. Expensive. Fragile.
- **Blood-iron:** Absorbs mineral content from sacrificial blood. Banned. Found in deep mines and desert ruins.
- **Storm-jars:** Hold static charge for weeks. Common in League navy.

### Category Two: Debt Sinks

Objects that accept debt on the mage's behalf. Mostly banned as "debt-evasion artifacts." Confiscated and secretly studied.

- **Ash-urns:** Absorb thermal debt. When cracked, accumulated debt releases catastrophically.
- **Weeping stones:** Absorb water-debt; weep toxic brine. Eventually crumble.
- **Silence bells:** Absorb sound-debt. Permanently silenced after use.
- **Bone-charms:** Absorb mineral debt. Require mage's bone. Black market flourishes.
- **Nerve-coils:** Absorb neural debt. Single use. Reuse returns all accumulated damage.

### Category Three: Conduit Prosthetics

Allow borrowing from normally inaccessible sources. Mixed legality.

- **Leech-rings:** Borrow from another person's reservoir at distance. Banned.
- **Root-crowns:** Borrow water through tree networks. Slowly kills the trees.
- **Storm-kites:** Draw charge from clouds. Dangerous but legal.
- **Void-masks:** Borrow breath from others' lungs at line-of-sight. Banned.
- **Depth-weights:** Borrow from deep ocean pressure. Grey area; League uses openly.

### Category Four: Living Artifacts

Objects that were once alive, or still are.

- **Thrall-stock:** Conditioned humans serving as living reservoirs. Banned. Dominion priesthood uses in secret.
- **Hearth-cats:** Bred to run unnaturally hot. Small living heat source. Unregulated.
- **Salt-spouses:** People with rare condition converting debt into salt on their skin. Legal in Hold-Kingdoms. Transactional marriages.
- **Cradle-hounds:** Order monopoly. Detection, not reservoir replacement.

### Category Five: Precursor Artifacts

Objects from before the Order. From before current civilization. Do not officially exist. Confiscated and buried in the vault.

- **Debt-nulls:** Obsidian discs. Working performed while holding one generates no debt. Breaks the First Principle. Impervious to all known force.
- **Gate-keys:** Allow working outside one's Gate. The Order claims they destroy the original Gate. This is a lie.
- **Resonant bones:** Amplify spell effect without increasing debt. Not human. Not animal. Multiple stored in the vault.
- **The Still Children:** Petrified corpses. Savants read them and see nothing—not a blank, but an *absence*. Seven found. Stored separately. No Curator will discuss them.
